# Privacy Policy for Workspace

This page describes how **Workspace** specifically handles privacy and data protection. For general provisions including contact information, your rights, and how to exercise them, see our [Privacy Policy](/legal/privacy).

## Collaborative Content

Workspace is built for teams. Content you create, upload, or edit inside a workspace is visible to other members of that workspace based on the permissions you or your administrator set.

By default, workspace owners and administrators have broad access to content for management and compliance purposes. Regular members can access only the projects, files, and conversations they have been invited to.

## Shared Workspaces

When you join a workspace, your profile information, including your name and email address, becomes visible to other members. Your activity inside the workspace, such as edits, comments, and status changes, is visible according to the workspace's visibility settings.

Workspace owners can remove members at any time. Removed members lose access to workspace content immediately. Their past contributions remain visible unless the owner explicitly deletes them.

## User-Generated Content

You retain ownership of the content you create in Workspace. We do not claim any rights over your documents, designs, code, or other materials. Processing this content happens solely to provide the Workspace service.

We do not scan user-generated content for advertising purposes. Malware, spam, or policy violations may be scanned to keep the platform safe. You control who can view, edit, or share the content you create.

## Team Data

Workspace administrators can view team-level data, including member lists, usage statistics, and billing information. Admins can also configure integrations, set permissions, and manage data retention policies for the workspace.

Individual members can see their own activity and any content shared with them. Members cannot see private content belonging to other members unless explicitly shared.
